About

Dr. Benjamin Laffitte is an Assistant Professor at Chengdu University of Technology, where his research focuses on terrestrial carbon cycling through data-integrated modelling, machine learning, and deep learning.

His work combines field observations, tree-ring methods, synthesis datasets, remote sensing, and statistical modelling to investigate carbon dynamics in forest, urban forest, disturbed, restored, and high-latitude ecosystems.

Alongside his research, Benjamin has developed and taught environmental science modules for primary school students in Chengdu public parks and cultural institutions, including Jincheng Park, East Lake Park, and the Natural History Museum affiliated with Chengdu University of Technology. He has also provided research and analysis guidance to high school, undergraduate, and master’s students.

Benjamin earned his PhD under Professor Dr. Ya Tang at Sichuan University, where he studied the effects of elevated CO2 and pollutants on trees and forest ecosystems in Jiuzhaigou National Nature Reserve. He previously completed a master’s degree in Ecology and Anthropization at the Institut National Polytechnique, affiliated with Université Paul Sabatier, Toulouse 3, and a bachelor’s degree in General Ecology at Université Paul Sabatier, Toulouse 3.
